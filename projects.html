The Lunar Lander

November 2023

SystemVerilog, Field Programmable Gate Array

In the Lunar Lander Project involved a sophisticated design and development of a Moon Lander utilizing SystemVerilog and a FPGA (Field Programmable Gate Array). The project's core comprised four integral units:

1. Memory Unit: A repository for crucial flight data.

2. Arithmetic Unit: Responsible for critical computational tasks, ensuring accurate adjustments to the lander's trajectory.

3. Control Unit: This unit was pivotal in determining the lander's status, specifically monitoring for potential crash or land scenarios.

4. Display Unit: Visually presenting key parameters such as altitude, thrust, velocity, and fuel levels on the FPGA.

For this project, I leveraged sequential state machines for coherent workflow management, meticulously integrating modules corresponding to each unit. Additionally, my approach included the strategic utilization of SystemVerilog for implementing essential arithmetic operations, pivotal in the accurate functioning of the Lander and integrating the modules for each unit that completes the Lander.
